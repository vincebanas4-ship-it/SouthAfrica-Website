Festival Tourism in South Africa

South Africa is a nation that loves to celebrate. Throughout the year, the country comes alive with a spectacular calendar of festivals spanning arts and culture, music, food and wine, heritage, and community. Festival tourism in South Africa offers visitors a vibrant and immersive way to experience the country's extraordinary diversity, creativity, and spirit — from world-class international events to intimate local gatherings rooted in tradition.

  • National Arts Festival — Makhanda
    • The National Arts Festival held annually in Makhanda (formerly Grahamstown) in the Eastern Cape is the largest arts festival on the African continent and one of the top ten arts festivals in the world. Over 11 days, the town transforms into a buzzing hub of theatre, dance, music, visual arts, film, and street performances, drawing artists and audiences from across South Africa and beyond.
  • Cape Town International Jazz Festival
    • The Cape Town International Jazz Festival, held annually at the Cape Town International Convention Centre, is Africa's greatest jazz celebration and one of the top jazz festivals in the world. Known as "Africa's Grandest Gathering," it attracts over 37,000 visitors across two nights and features more than 40 local and international jazz, soul, and world music artists performing across multiple stages.
  • Oppikoppi Music Festival
    • Oppikoppi is South Africa's most iconic outdoor music festival, held annually on a farm near Northam in Limpopo. Known for its dusty, raw atmosphere and eclectic mix of rock, alternative, and indie music, it has been a beloved fixture of the South African music calendar since 1994. The festival draws tens of thousands of music lovers who camp under the stars for a long weekend of live performances.
  • Hermanus Whale Festival
    • The Hermanus Whale Festival, held every September along the Western Cape coastline, is the only whale festival in the world to be awarded the prestigious environmental Green Flag. The festival celebrates the annual arrival of southern right whales in Walker Bay and features live music, food stalls, arts and crafts, environmental education, and spectacular whale watching from the cliff paths above the sea.
  • Franschhoek Bastille Festival
    • The Franschhoek Bastille Festival, held each July in the Cape Winelands, celebrates the French Huguenot heritage of the valley with a weekend of fine food, world-class wine, live entertainment, and street celebrations. Visitors dressed in red, white, and blue fill the charming streets of Franschhoek for one of the most elegant and festive events on the South African cultural calendar.
  • Cape Town Carnival
    • The Cape Town Carnival, held annually along the Green Point Fan Walk, is a spectacular celebration of South Africa's cultural diversity and creative spirit. Thousands of performers in elaborate costumes parade through the streets in a dazzling display of colour, music, and dance, drawing hundreds of thousands of spectators and celebrating what it means to be part of the Rainbow Nation.
