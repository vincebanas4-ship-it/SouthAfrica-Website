Heritage Tourism in South Africa

South Africa's heritage is as layered as its landscapes — shaped by ancient indigenous civilisations, colonial encounters, the struggle against apartheid, and the triumph of democracy. Heritage tourism invites visitors to walk through living history, explore UNESCO World Heritage Sites, and connect with the stories that have defined one of the world's most remarkable nations.

  • Robben Island
    • Robben Island, a UNESCO World Heritage Site off the coast of Cape Town, is where Nelson Mandela was imprisoned for 18 of his 27 years behind bars. Today it stands as a powerful symbol of the struggle for freedom and the resilience of the human spirit, drawing visitors from across the globe.
  • Cradle of Humankind
    • Located northwest of Johannesburg, the Cradle of Humankind is a UNESCO World Heritage Site containing some of the world's most significant fossil hominid sites. The Maropeng Visitor Centre and Sterkfontein Caves offer fascinating insights into the origins of the human species dating back over three million years.
  • Apartheid Museum
    • The Apartheid Museum in Johannesburg is widely regarded as one of the most powerful museums in the world. Through photographs, film footage, and personal accounts, it chronicles the rise and fall of apartheid and celebrates South Africa's hard-won path to democracy.
  • Voortrekker Monument
    • The Voortrekker Monument in Pretoria commemorates the Afrikaner pioneers who left the Cape Colony in the Great Trek of the 1830s and 1840s. It is one of South Africa's most visited heritage sites and houses a museum with intricate friezes depicting Voortrekker history and culture.
  • uKhahlamba-Drakensberg Park
    • A dual UNESCO World Heritage Site, the uKhahlamba-Drakensberg Park is celebrated both for its breathtaking mountain scenery and its extraordinary collection of San rock art. With over 35,000 individual rock paintings, it contains the largest and most concentrated group of San paintings in Africa.
  • Freedom Park & Union Buildings
    • Freedom Park in Pretoria is a national heritage site dedicated to the memory of all who lost their lives in the conflicts that shaped South Africa. Nearby, the Union Buildings — the official seat of the South African government — overlook the city and house a iconic statue of Nelson Mandela on its grounds.
