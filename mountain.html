Mountain Tourism in South Africa

South Africa's mountain ranges are among its most dramatic and awe-inspiring natural features. From the iconic silhouette of Table Mountain to the towering peaks of the Drakensberg, the country's highlands offer world-class hiking, breathtaking scenery, rich biodiversity, and a profound sense of wilderness. Mountain tourism in South Africa is an invitation to explore some of the most spectacular landscapes on the African continent.

  • Table Mountain, Cape Town
    • Table Mountain is one of the world's most recognisable natural landmarks and a proud member of the New Seven Wonders of Nature. Visitors can hike one of many scenic trails to the flat-topped summit or take the revolving cable car for panoramic views of Cape Town, the Atlantic Ocean, and the Cape Peninsula stretching far below.
  • Drakensberg Mountains, KwaZulu-Natal
    • The Drakensberg — meaning "Dragon's Mountain" in Afrikaans — is South Africa's highest mountain range and a UNESCO World Heritage Site. Stretching over 1,000 kilometres, it offers multi-day hikes, rock climbing, horse riding, and access to thousands of ancient San rock art paintings set within the stunning uKhahlamba-Drakensberg Park.
  • Cederberg Wilderness Area
    • The Cederberg in the Western Cape is a rugged, remote mountain wilderness renowned for its bizarre rock formations, crystal-clear rock pools, and ancient San rock art. It is a paradise for hikers, rock climbers, and stargazers — the area has some of the darkest skies in South Africa, making it ideal for astronomy tourism.
  • Magaliesberg Mountains, Gauteng
    • The Magaliesberg is one of the oldest mountain ranges on Earth, located just west of Johannesburg and Pretoria. It is a popular weekend escape for city dwellers, offering hiking trails, zip-lining, hot air ballooning, and a variety of scenic lodges and spas nestled among the rocky kloofs and riverine valleys.
  • Swartberg Mountains, Western Cape
    • The Swartberg Mountains form part of the Cape Fold Belt and are a UNESCO World Heritage Site within the Cape Floral Region. The legendary Swartberg Pass, built by Thomas Bain in the 1880s, winds dramatically through the range and is considered one of the finest mountain passes in the world, drawing motorists and cyclists from across the globe.
  • Sani Pass, KwaZulu-Natal
    • Sani Pass is a legendary mountain route ascending the Drakensberg escarpment into the Kingdom of Lesotho. Accessible only by 4x4 vehicle, it offers thrilling switchback roads, dramatic cliff-edge views, and at the top, the highest pub in Africa at over 2,874 metres above sea level — a bucket-list experience for adventure travellers.
