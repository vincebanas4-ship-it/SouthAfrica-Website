Eco Tourism in South Africa

South Africa is one of the world's most biodiverse countries, home to an extraordinary variety of ecosystems — from lush indigenous forests and sweeping wetlands to dramatic coastlines and semi-arid deserts. Eco tourism in South Africa offers responsible, sustainable travel experiences that connect visitors with nature while supporting conservation efforts and local communities.

  • iSimangaliso Wetland Park
    • South Africa's first UNESCO World Heritage Site, iSimangaliso Wetland Park in KwaZulu-Natal spans over 280,000 hectares of lakes, swamps, beaches, and coral reefs. It is home to hippos, crocodiles, leatherback turtles, and hundreds of bird species, making it one of Africa's most extraordinary eco tourism destinations.
  • Tsitsikamma National Park
    • Stretching along the Garden Route, Tsitsikamma National Park encompasses ancient indigenous forests, dramatic cliffs, and the pristine Storms River. Visitors can explore canopy tours through the forest, hike the famous Otter Trail, and snorkel in the protected marine reserve along the rugged coastline.
  • Boulders Beach Penguin Colony
    • Located near Simon's Town on the Cape Peninsula, Boulders Beach is home to a thriving colony of African penguins — one of only a few places on Earth where visitors can observe these endangered seabirds up close in their natural habitat. The colony is protected within the Table Mountain National Park.
  • Richtersveld National Park
    • The Richtersveld, a UNESCO World Heritage Site in the Northern Cape, is a rugged mountainous desert renowned for its remarkable plant diversity, including the world's largest succulent plant species. It is a remote wilderness offering guided eco tours, 4x4 trails, and canoe trips along the Orange River.
  • Cape Floral Region
    • The Cape Floral Region is one of the world's six floral kingdoms and a UNESCO World Heritage Site, containing over 9,000 plant species — nearly 70% of which are found nowhere else on Earth. Kirstenbosch National Botanical Garden in Cape Town is the gateway to this extraordinary biome, beloved by botanists and nature lovers alike.
  • Whale Watching in Hermanus
    • Hermanus on the Western Cape coast is internationally recognised as one of the best land-based whale watching destinations in the world. Southern right whales migrate to Walker Bay between June and November to calve and nurse their young, drawing thousands of eco tourists to witness this spectacular natural event each year.
