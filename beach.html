Beach Tourism in South Africa

South Africa is blessed with over 2,500 kilometres of stunning coastline, bordered by two oceans — the Atlantic to the west and the Indian Ocean to the east. From the icy, dramatic shores of the Western Cape to the warm, tropical beaches of KwaZulu-Natal, the country offers an exceptional variety of beach experiences for every type of traveller.

  • Camps Bay Beach, Cape Town
    • Camps Bay is one of Cape Town's most iconic beaches, set against the backdrop of the Twelve Apostles mountain range. With its white sand, clear Atlantic waters, and vibrant beachfront strip of restaurants and cafés, it is a favourite destination for both locals and international visitors seeking sun, scenery, and style.
  • Clifton Beaches, Cape Town
    • Clifton's four sheltered beaches — numbered First through Fourth — are among the most sought-after in South Africa. Protected from the wind by granite boulders and backed by luxury apartments, they offer calm, crystal-clear waters and some of the most spectacular sunsets on the Cape Peninsula.
  • Durban's Golden Mile
    • Durban's Golden Mile is a stretch of warm Indian Ocean beaches running along the city's beachfront. Known for its excellent surfing conditions, safe swimming, and lively promenade packed with entertainment venues, restaurants, and water parks, it is the most visited beach destination in South Africa.
  • Jeffrey's Bay
    • Jeffrey's Bay, or J-Bay, on the Eastern Cape is world-famous for its perfect surf breaks and is consistently ranked among the top surfing destinations on the planet. Beyond surfing, its long stretches of white sand, laid-back atmosphere, and abundance of seashells make it a beloved coastal retreat for beach lovers of all kinds.
  • Plettenberg Bay
    • Nestled along the Garden Route, Plettenberg Bay is one of South Africa's most beautiful beach towns. Its sweeping lagoon beaches, turquoise waters, and opportunities for dolphin and whale watching make it a premier holiday destination. Robberg Beach is particularly renowned for its pristine natural environment and nature reserve.
  • Sodwana Bay, KwaZulu-Natal
    • Sodwana Bay, located within the iSimangaliso Wetland Park, is regarded as one of the best scuba diving and snorkelling destinations in Africa. Its warm Indian Ocean waters are home to vibrant coral reefs, whale sharks, manta rays, and hundreds of tropical fish species, attracting divers and marine enthusiasts from around the world.
