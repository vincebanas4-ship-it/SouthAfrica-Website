Religious Tourism in South Africa

South Africa's rich and diverse spiritual landscape makes it one of Africa's most compelling destinations for religious tourism. As a nation where Christianity, Islam, Hinduism, Judaism, and indigenous African spiritual traditions coexist, South Africa offers faith-based travellers a remarkable variety of sacred sites, pilgrimage routes, historic places of worship, and vibrant religious festivals. From grand cathedrals and ornate mosques to sacred mountains and ancient ceremonial sites, religious tourism in South Africa is a deeply moving journey through faith, history, and culture.

Christianity is the largest religion in South Africa, with over 85% of the population identifying as Christian according to national surveys. Among Christian denominations, Protestantism is the most widely followed, accounting for approximately 36% of the total population. The Zion Christian Church (ZCC) is the largest African-initiated church in the country, with millions of members primarily from the Black African community. Other significant Christian denominations include the Roman Catholic Church (7.1%), the Methodist Church, the Dutch Reformed Church, and the Anglican Church. Outside of Christianity, African Traditional Religions are practised by approximately 5–15% of the population, while Islam accounts for about 1.9%, Hinduism for about 1%, and Judaism for a small but historically significant minority. South Africa's constitution guarantees full freedom of religion to all its citizens.

  • Zion City Moria — Zion Christian Church Pilgrimage, Limpopo
    • Zion City Moria in Limpopo is one of the most significant religious pilgrimage sites in Africa and the spiritual home of the Zion Christian Church (ZCC), one of the largest African-initiated churches on the continent. Each year at Easter, millions of ZCC pilgrims make the journey to Moria to worship, seek healing, and experience a profound sense of communal faith. The sheer scale and spiritual intensity of the Easter gathering make it one of the most extraordinary religious events in the Southern Hemisphere.
  • St George's Cathedral & the Cape Kramats, Cape Town
    • Cape Town offers a remarkable concentration of sacred sites spanning multiple faiths. St George's Cathedral in the city centre, famously associated with Archbishop Desmond Tutu and the anti-apartheid struggle, is a landmark of Anglican heritage and social justice. Equally significant are the Cape Kramats — a ring of five Muslim holy shrines encircling the Cape Peninsula, built over the graves of revered Islamic scholars and leaders brought to the Cape as slaves or political exiles. Muslim pilgrims visit these kramats year-round as an act of spiritual devotion.
  • Nazareth Baptist Church — Ebuhleni, KwaZulu-Natal
    • The Nazareth Baptist Church, also known as the Shembe Church, is one of South Africa's most distinctive and spiritually vibrant African-initiated churches, founded in the early 20th century by the prophet Isaiah Shembe. Its sacred headquarters at Ebuhleni near Durban draws hundreds of thousands of devoted pilgrims for the annual Shembe festivals, during which traditional Zulu song, dance, white robes, and fervent prayer combine in a uniquely South African expression of faith that has captivated observers from around the world.
  • Emmanuel Cathedral & Hindu Temples, Durban
    • Durban is home to one of the largest Indian communities outside of India, making it a vibrant centre of Hindu and Muslim religious life. The magnificent Emmanuel Cathedral, a Roman Catholic landmark in the heart of the city, stands alongside some of the most elaborate Hindu temples in the Southern Hemisphere, including the colourful Hare Krishna Temple of Understanding and the ancient Sri Siva Subramaniar Alayam temple. Durban's religious diversity makes it a compelling destination for interfaith religious tourism.
  • The South African Jewish Museum & Great Synagogue, Cape Town
    • The South African Jewish Museum in Cape Town, located adjacent to the historic Great Synagogue, is one of the finest Jewish heritage museums in the world. It traces the history of the South African Jewish community from their origins in Lithuania and Eastern Europe to their profound contribution to South African arts, commerce, and the anti-apartheid movement. The adjacent Holocaust and Genocide Centre provides deeply moving testimony to the importance of human rights and collective memory. Together, these sites form a significant destination for Jewish religious and heritage tourism.
  • Sacred Natural Sites & Indigenous Spiritual Traditions
    • For many of South Africa's indigenous communities — including the Zulu, Xhosa, Sotho, and San peoples — sacred mountains, forests, rivers, and pools hold deep spiritual significance as sites of ancestral connection, healing, and ritual practice. These living sacred landscapes, which include sites such as the Ncome River in KwaZulu-Natal and various ancestral mountains in the Eastern Cape, offer respectful visitors a rare and humbling insight into African spirituality, animism, and the enduring bond between people, land, and ancestry that lies at the heart of traditional South African culture.
