Sports Tourism in South Africa

South Africa is a nation deeply passionate about sport. From rugby and cricket to football and athletics, the country has hosted some of the world's most prestigious sporting events and produced legendary athletes who have inspired generations. Sports tourism in South Africa offers fans and participants alike the chance to experience world-class events, iconic stadiums, and thrilling competitions set against the backdrop of one of Africa's most vibrant nations.

  • Rugby — The Springboks
    • The Springboks are South Africa's national rugby team and one of the most celebrated rugby sides in the world, having won the Rugby World Cup four times. Watching a Springbok test match at iconic venues such as Ellis Park in Johannesburg or DHL Newlands in Cape Town is a bucket-list experience for any rugby fan visiting the country.
  • Football — 2010 FIFA World Cup Legacy
    • South Africa made history in 2010 as the first African nation to host the FIFA World Cup. The tournament left a lasting legacy of world-class stadiums including the iconic FNB Stadium — known as Soccer City — in Johannesburg, which remains one of the largest and most spectacular football venues on the African continent.
  • Cricket — Proteas
    • Cricket holds a special place in South African sporting culture. The Proteas, South Africa's national cricket team, are a formidable force in world cricket. Iconic venues such as Newlands Cricket Ground in Cape Town and SuperSport Park in Centurion offer spectacular settings for Test matches, One Day Internationals, and the popular SA20 T20 league.
  • Comrades Marathon
    • The Comrades Marathon is the world's oldest and largest ultramarathon, run annually between Durban and Pietermaritzburg in KwaZulu-Natal over a distance of approximately 90 kilometres. It attracts over 25,000 runners from around the world each year and is regarded as the ultimate test of human endurance, drawing massive crowds of spectators along the entire route.
  • Cape Town Cycle Tour
    • The Cape Town Cycle Tour is the world's largest individually timed cycling event, attracting over 35,000 cyclists annually. The 109-kilometre route winds through Cape Town's most scenic landscapes — past Table Mountain, along the Atlantic Seaboard, and around the Cape Peninsula — making it as visually spectacular as it is athletically demanding.
  • Golf — Home of Champions
    • South Africa has produced some of the greatest golfers in history, including Gary Player and Ernie Els. The country boasts over 400 golf courses set in stunning natural environments, from the bush courses of the Lowveld to the clifftop links of the Garden Route. The Nedbank Golf Challenge at Sun City is one of Africa's most prestigious annual tournaments.
