Wine Tourism in South Africa

South Africa has been producing wine for over 350 years, making it one of the oldest wine-producing nations in the New World. The Cape Winelands, set against dramatic mountain ranges and fertile valleys, offer world-class wine estates, cellar tours, and gourmet dining experiences. Wine tourism in South Africa is a journey through breathtaking scenery, rich history, and exceptional flavours.

  • Stellenbosch Wine Route
    • Stellenbosch is the heart of South Africa's wine industry and one of the most celebrated wine regions in the world. Surrounded by mountains and dotted with Cape Dutch architecture, it is home to over 150 wine estates offering tastings, cellar tours, and award-winning restaurants set among rolling vineyards.
  • Franschhoek Wine Valley
    • Known as the "Culinary Capital of South Africa," Franschhoek is a charming valley town founded by French Huguenot refugees in the late 17th century. Its wine estates produce some of the country's finest wines, and the famous Franschhoek Wine Tram offers a scenic tour through the valley's vineyards and cellars.
  • Paarl Wine Route
    • Paarl is one of the oldest towns in South Africa and home to a thriving wine route featuring diverse estates ranging from boutique family farms to large co-operatives. The region is particularly renowned for its Chenin Blanc, Shiraz, and Cabernet Sauvignon, produced in a warm, fertile valley setting.
  • Robertson Wine Valley
    • Robertson Wine Valley, situated along the Breede River, is known as the "Valley of Wine and Roses." It produces an impressive range of white wines and sparkling wines, and its many estates welcome visitors with warm hospitality, scenic picnic spots, and excellent value wine tastings throughout the year.
  • Constantia Wine Route
    • The Constantia Valley, nestled on the slopes of Table Mountain just outside Cape Town, is home to South Africa's oldest wine estates. Groot Constantia, founded in 1685, is the country's oldest wine-producing estate and a national heritage site. The cool, mist-fed climate produces elegant Sauvignon Blanc and Semillon wines.
  • Elgin & Hemel-en-Aarde Valley
    • Elgin and the Hemel-en-Aarde Valley near Hermanus are among South Africa's coolest wine-growing regions, producing internationally acclaimed Pinot Noir and Chardonnay. Surrounded by apple orchards and fynbos-covered mountains, these valleys offer a tranquil, off-the-beaten-path wine tourism experience of exceptional quality.
