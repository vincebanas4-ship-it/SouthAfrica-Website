Cruise Tourism in South Africa

South Africa's magnificent coastline stretches over 2,500 kilometres along two great oceans — the Atlantic and the Indian — offering cruise travellers a spectacular and diverse maritime experience unlike anywhere else on the African continent. With world-class port facilities in Cape Town, Durban, and Port Elizabeth, a warm and sunny cruise season running from November to April, and easy access to extraordinary wildlife, vineyards, cultural heritage sites, and natural wonders, South Africa has firmly established itself as one of the premier cruise destinations of the Southern Hemisphere.

  • Cape Town — The V&A Waterfront & Table Bay
    • Cape Town is South Africa's premier cruise port and one of the most celebrated port cities in the world. Ships dock at the Cape Town Cruise Terminal adjacent to the iconic Victoria & Alfred Waterfront, placing passengers within easy reach of Table Mountain, Robben Island, the Cape Winelands, the Cape of Good Hope, and the vibrant restaurants, galleries, and markets of one of Africa's most cosmopolitan cities. The dramatic backdrop of Table Mountain rising above Table Bay makes arriving in Cape Town by sea one of the most breathtaking maritime approaches anywhere on the globe.
  • Durban — The Indian Ocean Gateway
    • Durban is South Africa's busiest cruise port and the gateway to the warm waters of the Indian Ocean. The Durban Cruise Terminal is strategically located close to the city centre, allowing passengers to explore the famous Golden Mile beachfront, the vibrant Victoria Street Market, the colourful Indian Quarter, and uShaka Marine World with ease. Cruise lines operating Indian Ocean itineraries frequently include Durban as a key port of call, with routes extending to Mozambique, Madagascar, Mauritius, and the Seychelles.
  • Port Elizabeth (Gqeberha) — Gateway to the Garden Route & Addo
    • Port Elizabeth, officially renamed Gqeberha, serves as an important port of call on South African coastal cruise itineraries. Positioned at the heart of the Eastern Cape, it offers cruise passengers convenient access to the magnificent Addo Elephant National Park — home to more than 600 elephants — as well as the world-renowned Garden Route, the Tsitsikamma Forest, and the pristine beaches of Jeffreys Bay. The friendly, relaxed character of the city and its warm Algoa Bay waters make it a charming and rewarding port stop.
  • Indian Ocean Island Cruise Routes
    • South Africa serves as the ideal departure and arrival point for spectacular Indian Ocean island cruise itineraries. Major cruise lines including MSC Cruises, Costa Cruises, and Cunard operate routes from Durban and Cape Town that call at Mozambique, Madagascar, Réunion, Mauritius, and the Seychelles — a string of tropical island paradises offering turquoise lagoons, coral reefs, spice markets, and Creole culture. These routes combine the rich diversity of the African mainland with the idyllic beauty of the Indian Ocean in a single, unforgettable voyage.
  • The Union-Castle Line — South Africa's Cruise Heritage
    • South Africa has a proud and storied maritime heritage, most notably associated with the legendary Union-Castle Line, which operated a regular passenger mail service between Southampton and South African ports from 1900 until 1977. For generations of South Africans and British travellers, the distinctive lavender-hulled Castle liners were a beloved symbol of sea travel, connecting families across continents. The history of the Union-Castle Line — and the era of great ocean liner travel it represented — remains a cherished chapter in South African maritime memory, celebrated through museums and maritime heritage collections across the country.
  • Cruise Season & Practical Information
    • The South African cruise season runs primarily from November to April, coinciding with the Southern Hemisphere summer, when warm temperatures, calm seas, and long daylight hours create ideal conditions for coastal cruising. Most international cruise lines position ships in South African waters during this period, offering a wide range of itineraries from short three-night coastal hops to extended two-week voyages encompassing multiple Indian Ocean destinations. Cruise passengers entering South Africa require a valid passport, and visa requirements vary by nationality — visitors are advised to check entry requirements with the South African Department of Home Affairs well in advance of travel.
